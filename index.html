02 — The Solution

A wireless delivery layer
for stranded energy.

FIG. 02A Insert diagram — transmitter to receiver beam path
A / How it works

A focused microwave beam, point‑to‑point.

A flat phased-array transmitter converts renewable electricity into a focused 2.45 GHz microwave beam. A rectenna at the destination converts it back to usable electricity at 80–85% efficiency3 — no wires, no grid connection required.

FIG. 02B Insert graphic — phased array element drift visualization
B / The hard problem

Phased arrays drift.

Heat, aging, and weather cause each element's timing to shift, scattering the beam and destroying efficiency. Today's fix is to take the system offline for days of manual recalibration.

FIG. 02C Insert graphic — AI calibration loop on pilot signal
C / BeamerBoy's IP

Continuous AI self‑calibration.

Our AI self-calibration system reads the pilot signal the receiver already sends, detects drift at each element in real-time, and corrects continuously — while the system is actively transmitting. No downtime. No lab. No degradation.

06 — About Us

A delivery problem,
not a generation problem.

BeamerBoy was founded on a simple observation: the world's clean energy problem is not a generation problem — it is a delivery problem. Billions of dollars of renewable infrastructure sits stranded because the grid can't move energy fast enough. We're building the wireless delivery layer that fixes this.

We are a student team working at the intersection of RF engineering, machine learning, and energy systems. We believe the most important infrastructure companies of the next decade will not build wires — they will make wires unnecessary.
